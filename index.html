EXPERIENCE

After graduation in 1986 with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering degree from the University of Michigan (Magna Cum Laude), I was employed at Allied-Signal Bendix Aerospace (South Bend, IN) in the test engineering department.  This division designed aircraft engine controls and our department integrated systems that simulated aircraft engine signals used to test the engine control designs.  In addition to hardware-oriented tasks I also performed software development that involved assembly language programming for Texas Instruments TMS99000 microprocessors.

I moved back to Ann Arbor, MI after accepting a position as a test engineer at Xycom Automation Inc.  Xycom designed and manufactured an extensive product line of electronic circuit boards based on the VMEbus computer architecture and a second product line of ruggedized, industrial PC computer systems.  As an engineer I developed software in both C and assembly language (Intel and Motorola processors) to perform product functionality testing.

After 2.5 years in the test engineering department I accepted a different position at Xycom as an application engineer in the VMEbus products group.  My primary responsibilities focused on assisting customers with integrating our circuit boards into their systems.  This support included working with their software code during development for a wide range of applications.

After the above 6.5 year period I made a significant career change and became a sales engineer for companies including Rathsburg Associates, Allen-Bradley DataMyte, Microtek Controls and SBS Technologies.  I was involved in both the sales of hardware and software products.

In my last position as a sales engineer I had a territory that covered 13 states.  I found the significant amount of travel involved undesirable and was "ready for a change".  Eventually I ended up following an entrepreneurial path and started my own business based on my passion for woodworking.  The business eventually led to me productizing the decorative stencils that I used in painting my woodworking products.  I initially sold these stencils on eBay and based on the strong results created my own ecommerce web site to sell them.  I developed this site using Microsoft FrontPage and used a table-based layout .  I also had an opportunity to use my programming skills when I developed a Visual Basic program that automated multiple tasks that allowed me to simultaneously create stencil designs (Adobe Illustrator files), generate associated HTML pages for the web site and update an Excel spreadsheet with product details.  I discontinued operating this business in 2016.

As documented and demonstrated in other sections, I have subsequently gained a significant amount of knowledge and experience in a wide range of both front-end and back-end software development technologies in addition to earning the credential of Oracle Certified Professional after passing the Java SE6 Exam.

Shortly before the COVID-19 pandemic started I was in the process of seeking a position as a software developer.  Because of the significant heath concerns and economic instability that resulted as the pandemic spread across the globe I discontinued my employment search.  Now that we are in the waning days of the pandemic along with effective vaccinations and treatments I am resuming my search efforts.  During this prolonged waiting time period I have made significant efforts to keep my knowledge and skills current and proficient.
